Seminar 1

The United Kingdom: General Outline

  1. Read the material from the textbooks indicated below and be ready to give brief talks on the following issues.
    1. Different names referring to the UK (Great Britain, the British Isles, Albion, Britannia).
    2. Political division of the UK.
    3. Population of the UK, its distribution and density.
    4. National symbols identifying each of the countries.
    5. Languages in Britain.
    6. Religion.
    7. Geography of the UK:
      1. 7.1. The coastline (islands, seas, channels).
      2. 7.2. Relief (mountains, highland and lowlands).
      3. 7.3. Rivers and lakes.
      4. 7.4. Climate and the main factors that influence it.
      5. 7.5. Advantages and disadvantages of the geographical position.
    8. Economy of the UK:
      1. 8.1. Mineral wealth.
      2. 8.2. Major industries.
      3. 8.3. Main industrial regions and cities.
      4. 8.4. New tendencies in the economy.
  2. Study the map of Britain and label the following places:
    1. countries of the UK;
    2. capital cities;
    3. the backbone of England;
    4. the grassy hills across the north end of the Pennines;
    5. the district of beautiful lakes;
    6. the most westerly and southerly points of Great Britain;
    7. the mountains that cover practically all the territory of Wales;
    8. the three parts into which Scotland is geographically divided;
    9. the highest summit of the Grampian Mountains;
    10. the mountains in the north-east of Northern Ireland;
    11. the famous basalt columns in Northern Ireland.
  3. Choose a topic for your project work and prepare a presentation. Think over the questions for discussion at class.
    1. Iconic places in England (Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland).
    2. Iconic people of England (Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland).
