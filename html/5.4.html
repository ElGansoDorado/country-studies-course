Seminar 4

The UK State and Political Life

  1. Read the material from the textbooks indicated below and be ready to give brief talks on the following issues.
    1. Britain as a constitutional monarchy:
      1. 1.1. Peculiarities of the British Constitution.
      2. 1.2. Separation of powers under the British Constitution.
      3. 1.3. The Head of the State. The King’s duties.
    2. The British Parliament:
      1. 2.1. Beginnings of Parliament.
      2. 2.2. The House of Commons.
      3. 2.3. The House of Lords.
      4. 2.4. Parliamentary electoral system.
      5. 2.5. Parliamentary procedure.
      6. 2.6. Passing laws.
    3. The Executive power. The Government:
      1. 3.1 The Prime Minister and his functions.
      2. 3.2. The Cabinet.
      3. 3.3. The present-day Prime Minister and his policy.
    4. Traditions of the British Parliament.
    5. Political parties of Great Britain:
      1. 5.1. The Conservative Party.
      2. 5.2. The Labour Party.
      3. 5.3. The Liberal Democratic Party.
      4. 5.4. The Welsh, Scottish and Irish Nationalist parties.
  2. Choose a topic for your project work and prepare a presentation. Think over the questions for discussion at class.
    1. British monarchy today.
    2. The Commonwealth of Nations.
    3. The constitutional reform in Britain: devolution.
    4. The House of Hanover.
    5. The House of Windsor.
